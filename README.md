# BarrelHouse

**BarrelHouse** is a dark craft-style homebrewing management app designed to track mashes, distilling stages, ageing, bottled stock, cellar inventory, shelf stock, photos, timelines, and production progress.

It turns messy homebrew notes, bottle counts, batch stages, and cellar chaos into a clean operational command centre.

---

## About the Project

BarrelHouse was built to solve a real household workflow: managing homemade spirits, mashes, ageing products, bottled stock, and cellar records in one practical place.

Instead of relying on scattered notes, memory, screenshots, or rough estimates, BarrelHouse gives users a structured way to see what is fermenting, what is ageing, what is bottled, what is on the shelf, and what needs attention next.

The app is designed with a dark barrelhouse-inspired interface, visual record cards, photo support, production tracking, and stock movement between different stages.

---

## Core Features

- Track active mashes and fermenting batches
- Manage distilled products ageing on wood chips or essence
- Record bottled products and finished stock
- Move stock between Cellar and Shelf
- Track bottle counts, litres, product types, and production progress
- Add photos to records
- View production timelines and stage history
- Monitor stock snapshots and operation summaries
- Edit and delete saved records
- Support private user data through login/logout functionality

---

## App Sections

### Dashboard

The dashboard gives a quick operational snapshot of BarrelHouse, including active batches, pending stock, shelf stock, cellar stock, due soon items, and production analytics.

### Batches

Used for mashes and washes that are currently fermenting.

### Urns

Used for distilled products that are ageing, resting, filtering, or sitting on wood chips or essence.

### Shelf

Used for products that are open, ready to drink, available, sold, or gifted.

### Cellar

Used for bottled stock that is stored, ageing, or waiting to be moved to the shelf.

### Timeline

Shows production progress across the full lifecycle of a batch or product.

## Design Direction

BarrelHouse uses a dark craft-led visual style inspired by timber, barrels, amber tones, cellar notes, and production ledgers.

The goal is for the app to feel practical, masculine, organised, and hands-on — less like a generic spreadsheet, and more like a working homebrew command centre.

---

## Why I Built It

I built BarrelHouse because I wanted a tool that matched the way homebrewing actually happens: batches overlap, bottles move around, stock gets opened, products age over time, and details are easy to forget.

This project helped me practise app design, product thinking, user workflows, data structure, and real-world problem solving.

It is also part of my larger learning journey into software development, app building, and building practical tools from real life.
